### Strategic Alignment: Objectives and Key Results (OKRs)
The Product Manager defines, collaboratively sets, tracks, and reports on Objectives and Key Results (OKRs) to ensure alignment between team efforts and overall product strategy. Regular progress monitoring and adjustments are crucial.

### Product Performance: Key Product Metrics
The Product Manager identifies, tracks, and analyzes key product metrics to assess performance, pinpoint areas for improvement, and inform future development decisions. Consider using a framework(s) like HEART, AARM, and/or North Star to guide your metric selection.

Examples of key product metrics include:

- User engagement (e.g., daily/monthly active users, session duration)
- Conversion rates
- Customer satisfaction (CSAT)
- Feature usage

### Software Delivery Performance: Predictability and Efficiency
The Product Manager monitors key metrics related to software delivery performance, drawing upon established industry benchmarks and best practices. The key metrics identified by the [annual DevOps Research and Assessment study](https://www.devops-research.com/research.html) that are particularly important:
- **Lead Time:** Time from code commit to successful production deployment.
- **Deployment Frequency:** How often code is deployed to production.
- **Change Failure Rate:** Percentage of changes resulting in degraded service.
- **Time to Restore Service:** Time to restore service after an incident or defect.

Additionally, depending on the chosen software development methodology (e.g., Kanban, Scrum), specific metrics are tracked:
- **Kanban (Continuous Flow):** Cycle time, throughput, work in progress (WIP).
- **Scrum (Sprint Predictability):** Velocity, sprint burndown, lead time, work in progress (WIP).
