## Scoping Session

### What is a Scoping Session & Why Should I Have One?

* A scoping session is the first step in the product lifecycle. Some might say it is a precursor to the product
  lifecycle.
* The primary purpose of a scoping is to create a shared understanding of goals, persona(s), flows and what you aim to
  accomplish with stakeholders, product team and dependency teams.
* Document goals of the feature/product to ensure all parties are clear on the problem and why solving the problem adds
  value to Platform+. If a solution has been identified to solve the problem, it may include alignment around the
  solution including reviewing any evidence that suggests the proposed solution will lead to the desired outcome. This
  includes identifying and mapping assumptions and risks associated with problem and/or solution space.

### As a Facilitator of a Scoping Session What Do I Do?

#### Planning The Session

* Ensure key members join the scoping session to establish a common understanding to avoid issues down the road. This
  includes critical stakeholders, business subject matter experts (SMEs) and technical SMEs. Smaller groups are always
  better to create alignment however, team members delivering the work can benefit from hearing context and direction
  directly in such session. When in doubt, consider adding people that will add value to the discussion.
* Determine a time window: depending on complexity, scoping meetings can take 1.5 to 2 hours to complete.

#### How To Facilitate

* Remain neutral. The facilitator should have an unbiased view on the feature / product being scoped.
* Focus on Who, What When... The how will come later, after the team understands the scope.
* Don’t make delivery commitments in a scoping session but understand if there are hard dates for achieving some
  capability. This will help the product team manage timelines through scope and quality considerations.

## Discovery and Framing

Discovery and Framing is the first portion of the product lifecycle. During discovery the team is actively trying to
understand the problem space and the user needs. The team starts off discovering all the problems to be solved and
then hones in on the key problems that will generate the most value. During framing the team ideates on all the
potential technical solutions and then prioritizes the solutions that are most likely to generate user and business
value.

Activities/artifacts that may be used during discovery and framing include:
* Persona Definition
* User Journey Mapping
* User Story Mapping
* Assumption Mapping
* Problem Prioritization
* Lean Canvas
* Product Vision Board
* Stakeholder Mapping
* User Interviews
* Usability Testing
* Architecture Diagrams

## Product Lifecycle Governance

* Tech council approves product progression through the Kanban stages
* Product Manager completes the Tech Council template starting with approval to enter in to discovery and framing stage
  to progress through each Kanban stage
* Document is updated over time as the product requests progression through Kanban maturity stages.
* Key criteria to review at tech council:
    * Strategic Rationale – Does this product serve a strategic purpose for Platform+ and does it have reasonable chances
      for a successful outcome?
    * Exit Agreements – What criteria must be in place for this product to progress to the next maturity stage? Pay
      careful attention to any deviations from the standard exit criteria.
    * People Requested – does the headcount requested align to the capacity needs of the product and can it be contained
      within allotted Platform+ Tech capacity?
