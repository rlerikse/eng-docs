## Milestones

#### What are Milestones?

* Target dates for events that are aligned to high priority Platform+ initiatives
* Usually requires coordination across multiple product groups
* The scope of the milestone is typically well-defined and less flexible than OKR driven work
* Each milestone is owned by a single product group that will coordinate work across all product groups
* Milestone owner will declare milestone complete upon launching scope of work to production in coordination with go to
  market strategy

#### What Purpose Do Milestones Serve?

* Align priority across multiple teams to a clearly defined area of focus
* Formalize the planning and risk assessment of new feature deployments across teams
* Communicate more accurate, real-time assessment of the date that the milestone scope will be delivered

### Formal Milestone Process (Top 5 Milestones)

* Platform+ Tech leadership work together to align on high priority objectives. High priority objectives with a clear
  scope and dependencies across product groups will be designated as "Top 5 milestones"
* Milestone owner is chosen to create a single-page scoping document which outlines the bulk of the milestone. The
  document contents will be reviewed by Platform+ Tech leadership to ensure alignment on milestone definition.
* Milestone owner communicates what they are expecting of all people who will play a role in the milestone. All teams in
  Platform+ Tech should have at least knowledge of the milestone's existence and priority.
* Milestone owner leads small and large groups sessions so product teams can understand their obligation to milestone
  and enter work into Rally for purposes of high-level forecasting.
* The Milestone Summary page will inform the date that is communicated to Platform+ leadership through the Smartsheets
  application. Product managers maintain backlog in a timely manner and review their impact on the forecasted milestone
  date using the Milestone Summary page.
* Teams iteratively deliver user stories. The milestone owner will meet weekly with Platform+ Tech leadership to discuss
  status, timing, issues, risks, and help needed
* Milestone owner will leverage our go to market practices to ensure successful transition from software delivery to
  business operations. The completion of the milestone should include a celebration of everyone's hard work.

### Story Mapping to Identify MVP + Iterations/Releases

#### What & Why?

Story Mapping is a practice for translating a user journey into a prioritized product backlog. Epics/Sub-Epics are
broken down into user stories and occurs continuously throughout the product development lifecycle. This helps to
sequence user stories into a prioritized product backlog and assemble work into releases. The Story Map allows teams to
establish a common understanding about the functionality of a product.

#### When?

Typically, during an inception or a planned meeting to take a Journey Map and break it into smaller chunks of work
leading to a prioritized product backlog. Oftentimes, this activity follows from D&F outcomes but not always.

### Product Roadmap

* is a high-level visual summary that communicates product direction, priority, and progress to internal teams and
  external stakeholders.
* is a guide for executing the strategy of a product and communicates what we want to build to achieve our outcomes.
* should be a continuous process throughout the lifecycle of a product and always evolving.

#### Best Practices

* Focus on priority first
* Always be prioritizing
* Understand your audience when presenting your roadmap
* When communicating time ALWAYS add padding for unknowns
* Delay solutioning as long as is responsible – you don’t want your roadmap to be a series or solutions you’ve committed
  to
* Outcomes over outputs – include in your roadmap the outcome you are trying to achieve; not just the feature you plan
  to develop
