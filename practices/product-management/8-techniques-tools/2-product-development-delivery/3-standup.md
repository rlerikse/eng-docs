This document outlines the process for our Daily Stand-up meetings to ensure efficient and effective team communication and collaboration.

## Meeting Structure and Participants
Daily Stand-ups are short (typically under 15 minutes), focused meetings designed to improve team communication and collaboration. The primary goal is to ensure daily alignment towards achieving iteration goals.

**Attendees:**
- Software Engineers
- Product Manager
- Product Designer
- Go-to-Market Lead

**Optional Attendees:** Product Line Anchors and Managers may attend to provide support and guidance, but their primary role is to ensure the team maintains its autonomy. Their participation should not disrupt the team's flow.

**Meeting Schedule:** Daily Stand-ups are typically held in the morning, at a consistent time. Scheduling should consider team members in different time zones to ensure optimal participation.

## Product Manager Responsibilities
While facilitation can rotates among team members, the Product Manager plays a key role in ensuring the effectiveness of Daily Stand-ups. Their responsibilities include:

- Modeling concise and effective communication during their turns as facilitator.
- Observing team dynamics and addressing any recurring impediments or communication breakdowns.
- Using the information gathered during stand-ups to inform their decision-making and proactively address potential risks.
- Ensuring the team maintains focus on addressing blockers and making progress toward iteration goals.

## Stand-up Cadence: A Step-by-Step Process
Each Daily Stand-up follows this structured sequence for each team member:

- Yesterday: Briefly summarize completed tasks.
- Today: Outline planned tasks.
- Blockers: Highlight any obstacles hindering progress.

This structure should be followed consistently to ensure clarity and efficiency.

## Best Practices for Successful Daily Stand-ups
To maximize the effectiveness of Daily Stand-ups:

- **Timeboxing:** Strictly adhere to the 15-minute time limit.
- **Visual Aids:** Utilize a Kanban board or similar visual tool to track progress and identify bottlenecks.
- **Action Item Tracking:** Document any action items arising from the meeting, assigning owners and deadlines. Follow up on these in subsequent stand-ups.
- **Proactive Problem Solving:** Address roadblocks and impediments immediately during the stand-up whenever possible.
- **Concise Communication:** Encourage brevity and active listening; avoid side conversations.
- **Iterative Improvement:** Regularly assess the effectiveness of the stand-up process and make adjustments as needed.
- **Off-line Discussions:** Defer detailed discussions or problem-solving sessions that extend beyond the 15-minute timebox to separate meetings or asynchronous communication.
