This document outlines the process for our Iteration Planning Meetings (IPMs) to ensure efficient and effective planning for upcoming iterations.

## Meeting Structure and Participants
The IPM is a dedicated meeting for rigorously planning near-term work. Its primary goals are to achieve team alignment on prioritized work items and to establish accurate estimations for the iteration.

**Attendees:**
- Software Engineers
- Product Manager
- Product Designer
- Go-to-Market Lead

**Optional Attendees:** Product Line Managers and Anchors may attend to provide support and guidance, but their primary role is to ensure the team maintains its autonomy.

**Meeting Schedule:** The IPM is typically scheduled weekly, at a consistent day and time, to coincide with the start of each new iteration. The meeting is generally one hour long, but the duration may adjust based on the number and complexity of user stories. The meeting should be as short as necessary to achieve its objectives, minimizing time away from regular work.

## Product Manager Responsibilities
The Product Manager plays a crucial role in facilitating a successful IPM. Their responsibilities include:

- Scheduling the meeting at a consistent time.
- Preparing user stories (writing new stories or reviewing stories prepared by the Anchor).
- Ensuring stories clearly articulate user value and acceptance criteria.
- Identifying stories for review in the upcoming IPM (using a tag in Rally is recommended).
- Keeping the IPM focused and on track (deferring unclear stories to the next meeting).
- Making minor updates to stories during the IPM for better clarity.
- Encouraging discussion to resolve differences in estimations.
- Ensuring all team members have an opportunity to contribute.
- Prioritizing stories for the Software Engineers to pick up after the IPM.

## IPM Cadence: A Step-by-Step Process
Each IPM follows this structured sequence:

- **Story Review:** The owner of the user story presents it to the team.
- **Discussion and Clarification:** The team discusses the story, asks clarifying questions, and addresses any ambiguities.
- **Story Update:** The story owner or Product Manager makes any necessary updates for improved clarity.
- **Effort Estimation:** The team (or just the Software Engineers) collaboratively estimates the effort required for the story.
- **Estimation Resolution:** The team works together to resolve any differences in estimations.
- **Kanban Stage Advancement:** The story owner or Product Manager moves the story to the next stage in the Kanban workflow.
- **Next Story Review:** The team moves on to the next user story, repeating the process.

## Best Practices for Successful Iteration Planning
To maximize the effectiveness of IPMs:

- **Proactive Preparation:** User stories should be refined and estimated before the meeting.
- **Capacity Management:** Assign a story point capacity to each iteration and avoid exceeding it.
- **Risk and Dependency Management:** Identify and plan for risks and dependencies that could impact story completion.
- **Iterative Improvement:** At the end of each iteration, reflect on the planning process's effectiveness and make adjustments for future IPMs.
