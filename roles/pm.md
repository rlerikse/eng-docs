## Product Manager

Defines the very essence of a product to solve both customer and business problems by partnering and collaborating with
business stakeholders, software engineers, and product designers to bring the team on a journey to de-risk product
direction and turn a product vision into reality.

The Product Manager discovers what is valuable, usable, and feasible acting in the intersection of business, technology,
and user experience.

Successful product managers keep Lean thinking at their forefront and focus on creating continuous feedback loops to
reduce product risk. They have a strong pulse on the business landscape to determine how to rigorously prioritize
features to best suit the market conditions

### Responsibilities

#### Strategy

* Understand Product Vision and Market
* Ensure Value Proposition for the life of the product
* Focus on Product outcomes over outputs
* Follow Product governance and provide feedback
* Establish and track Product metrics

#### Execution

* Align Product & Business Goals OKRs
* Establish outcome-oriented product roadmap
* Establish Learn-Build-Measure cycles
* Prioritize backlog using metrics
* Establish and manage milestones
* Identify and manage dependencies
* Effectively communicate & align stakeholders to commitments
* Ensure positive Product & Team Health
* Leads team through Product Development Lifecycle (Kanban) stages

#### Practices

* Scoping Inception
* Estimation
* Retrospectives
* Continuous Improvement
* Continuous Discovery & Framing
* Participate in CoP
* People Allocation
* Team Health Checks
* Facilitation Ceremonies
* Iteration Planning

#### Plays

* Vision Statement / Board
* Governance Doc
* Value Prop Canvas
* OKR generation
* Scoping
* Stakeholder Mapping
* Journey Mapping
* Experimentation
* Story mapping
* Forming Product Metrics
* Team Health Checks
* Story Estimation
* Backlog Management
* Outcome oriented roadmaps

Note: There is a lot to do here. When there is too much work for one PM, an associate PM may help with the workload
while the lead PM is the primary point of contact with external stakeholders

## Product Line and Product Group Manager Roles

* Ensure the product vision and strategy for product line/group clearly connects with the vision of Platform+ Tech and
  clearly communicate the vision and strategy to individuals within product teams
* Work closely with the Product Line Anchors and Go to Market Leads to establish an inclusive Product Roadmap
* Ensure the Product Roadmap is understood and aligned across Platform+ Tech including identifying, tracking and
  over-communicating dependencies to enable faster delivery
* Drive alignment of OKRs between Platform+ business and technology stakeholders
* Collaborate to decompose work that spans across product lines ensuring alignment towards strategic outcomes
* Lead and provide mentoring and coaching for Product Managers in the product line
* Identify skill gaps and find learning opportunities to close those skill gaps
* Drive consistency of Product Manager best practice (e.g., workflow, iteration planning) across the Product Line
* Review the Product Team health and team dynamics addressing concerns as needed
* Ambassador to raise issues to leadership on behalf of the Product Line’s Product Managers

## Go To Market Lead (GTM)

* The Go To Market (GTM) Lead is a person within the Platform+ organization but external to the Platform+ Tech
  organization with a vested interest in the success of the software product
* The GTM Lead advocates for software driven business outcomes to be developed that support the Platform+ business
  strategy
* They provide domain subject expertise that gives context to the “Why” a feature is being developed
* They give feedback as features are developed to optimize the opportunity for successful outcomes
* They help coordinate factors external to software delivery that promote successful software outcomes
* While the GTM Lead advocates for feature prioritization and timing, this is ultimately determined by the PM working
  with the team
